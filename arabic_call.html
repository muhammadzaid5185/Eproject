Arabic Calligraphy Styles

Explore the beautiful diversity of Arabic script

Kufic Script

One of the oldest Arabic scripts, characterized by its geometric and angular forms. Originally used for early Qur'anic manuscripts and architectural inscriptions.

  • Geometric precision
  • Angular letterforms
  • Architectural applications

Naskh Script

A flowing, rounded script that became the standard for printing Arabic text. Easy to read and widely used in books and documents.

  • Rounded letterforms
  • High legibility
  • Modern printing standard

Diwani Script

An ornate script developed during the Ottoman period, known for its highly decorative and flowing style used in royal documents.

  • Highly decorative
  • Flowing connections
  • Royal correspondence

Thuluth Script

Considered one of the most elegant scripts, with bold, flowing lines. Used for important texts and architectural decoration.

  • Elegant proportions
  • Bold letterforms
  • Monumental inscriptions
