Greek Calligraphy Styles

Explore the classical precision of Greek scripts

Uncial Script

A majuscule script (all capital letters) used from the 3rd to 8th centuries. Characterized by rounded, bold letterforms ideal for manuscripts.

  • Rounded capitals
  • Bold strokes
  • Early manuscripts

Minuscule Script

A cursive script developed in the 9th century, combining uppercase and lowercase forms. More efficient for writing and copying texts.

  • Mixed case letters
  • Efficient writing
  • Scholarly texts

Byzantine Script

Developed during the Byzantine Empire, combining classical Greek elements with ornate decorative features for religious texts.

  • Religious manuscripts
  • Ornate decorations
  • Imperial documents

Classical Greek

The ancient Greek alphabet as used in classical Athens and during the Hellenistic period. Foundation of Western calligraphy.

  • Geometric precision
  • Philosophical texts
  • Historical importance
