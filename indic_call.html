Indic Calligraphy Styles

Explore the spiritual diversity of Indian scripts

Devanagari Script

One of the most widely used writing systems in India, characterized by its horizontal line connecting letters. Used for Hindi, Sanskrit, and many other languages.

  • Horizontal top line
  • Rounded letterforms
  • Sacred texts

Grantha Script

An ancient script used primarily for writing Sanskrit in Tamil Nadu. Combines features of both Devanagari and Tamil scripts.

  • Classical manuscripts
  • Religious texts
  • Historical importance

Tigalari Script

An ancient script from Karnataka, used for copying palm leaf manuscripts. Known for its elegant, flowing letterforms.

  • Palm leaf writing
  • Elegant curves
  • Traditional preservation

Bengali Script

Known for its distinctive rounded characters and horizontal line. Used for Bengali, Assamese, and Manipuri languages.

  • Rounded shapes
  • Poetic tradition
  • Literary heritage
